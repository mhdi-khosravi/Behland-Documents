# 🏛️ BehLand DAO – Governance Framework (Light DAO)

**Version:** Initial Accepted Framework  
**Status:** Active (Early-Stage Governance)

---

## 1. Introduction

This document defines the **official DAO governance framework** of the BehLand project.

After more than six months of active development, BehLand introduces this **Light DAO model** as the current reference for collective decision-making, proposal submission, and voting processes within the ecosystem.

This framework is designed to support **transparent, simple, and human-centric governance**, aligned with BehLand’s educational philosophy and current stage of growth.

---

## 2. What is a DAO in BehLand?

A DAO (Decentralized Autonomous Organization) in BehLand is a **collective decision-making mechanism** where proposals are submitted, reviewed, and voted on by eligible participants.

The DAO in BehLand focuses on **structural, operational, and ecosystem-level decisions**, not financial governance or speculation.

Core principles:
- One proposal at a time
- Clear approval or rejection
- No alternative options during voting
- Majority-based execution

---

## 3. Decision Flow

The DAO decision process follows these steps:

1. A proposal is submitted using a defined template  
2. The proposal is reviewed for clarity and scope  
3. The proposal is published for voting  
4. Eligible participants cast their vote  
5. If **more than 50% approve**, the proposal is accepted  
6. Accepted proposals move to execution  

---

## 4. Voting Options

All DAO votes are strictly binary:

- **Approve**
- **Reject**

There are:
- No multiple-choice options  
- No amendments during voting  
- No partial approvals  

Participants simply express whether they **accept or reject** the proposal as presented.

---

## 5. Types of DAO Participation

BehLand currently recognizes three DAO participation scopes:

### 5.1 Management DAO
- **Participants:** Core managers and decision-makers  
- **Scope:** Strategic and high-level decisions  

### 5.2 Team DAO
- **Participants:** Internal team members and contributors  
- **Scope:** Operational and execution-related decisions  

### 5.3 Community DAO
- **Participants:** Community members, users, and investors  
- **Scope:** Non-financial, ecosystem-wide decisions  

Each proposal must clearly specify **which DAO type** it belongs to.

---

## 6. DAO Proposal Requirements

Every proposal submitted for voting must include:

### 6.1 Proposal Title  
A short and clear title describing the proposal.

### 6.2 Decision Type  
Specify one:
- Management DAO  
- Team DAO  
- Community DAO  

### 6.3 Proposal Description  
A simple and clear explanation of what is being proposed.

### 6.4 Rationale  
Why this proposal is necessary and what problem it addresses.

### 6.5 Impact Analysis  
A balanced overview of:
- Positive impacts  
- Possible risks or negative impacts  

### 6.6 Affected Groups  
Identify impacted groups:
- Team  
- Users  
- Investors  
- Entire ecosystem  

---

## 7. Governance Rules

- Voting options are limited to **Approve / Reject**
- Proposal content **cannot be modified** after voting starts
- No alternative proposals during the same vote
- Approved proposals are considered **final and executable**
- Rejected proposals may be revised and resubmitted later

---

## 8. Evolution Clause

This document represents the **current executable version** of BehLand’s DAO governance.

It is **not the final form** of the DAO system.

Planned future improvements may include:
- Vote-weight mechanisms
- Pre-voting discussion phases
- Execution accountability tracking
- On-chain governance integration
- NTT-based participation or eligibility logic

These improvements are expected to evolve gradually over the next **6–24 months**, aligned with platform and community maturity.

---

## 9. Responsibility & Oversight

- **Project Manager**  
  Responsible for implementing, maintaining, and operating DAO processes.

- **Founder / Ecosystem Lead**  
  Responsible for ensuring DAO decisions align with BehLand’s long-term vision.

Until a fully decentralized governance system is implemented, this document remains the **official and authoritative reference** for DAO-based decision-making within the BehLand project.
